Reflections on Prototyping

Posted by Xu on Oct 5, 2019

Beaudouin-Lafon and MacKay’s chapter on Prototyping Tools and Techniques clearly introduces classic prototyping methods while Li et al’s paper about "Topiary" -- a tool for prototyping location-enhanced applications -- talks about more innovative prototyping which has totally new forms. In this blog, I am going to talk about which prototyping methods are more promising in being applied to our own project -- the DogChat.

Mock ups with paper prototypes

Our project "DogChat" is an app-based project, aiming to help dog owners better understand their dogs' needs. Under this situation, paper prototypes would be one of the most suitable ways to do mock ups and participantary design. Here are some reasons why I choose paper prototypes. First of all, paper prototypes allow us to quickly sketch out the visual elements for user interface, which can be rapidly demonstrated to interviewees. Secondly, paper prototypes are easy to be modified, which is of great benefits for future design iterations.

Wizard-of-Oz with screen-based prototypes

Screen-based prototypes would be the advanced version of our paper prototypes. After paper prototype testing, we will design screen-based prototypes with some design tools like Figma, and operate the screen shots using wizard-of-oz methods. In this way, users will have a better idea of what the final app will look like. At the same time, wizard-of-oz method allows users to have some actual interaction experiences. This will greatly help us design in terms of better user experiences.
