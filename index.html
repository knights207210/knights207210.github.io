Alexa Skills Evaluation

Sikuli Lab, University of Colorado Boulder

In this study, I developed a voice crawler using python and speech-text converter packages to imtate actual human's interaction with Alexa devices and collect responses from 100 most popular Alexa skills under 10 different categories. Then these responses were evaluated to find out how they comply with current 13 voice skills design guidelines published by Amazon. Based on our findings and real skill examples, we offer suggestions for new guidelines to complement the existing ones and propose agendas for future HCI research to improve voice skills' user experiences. (paper under review)

E

E-Health Tutorial for Elder People using Browser Extension

Sikuli Lab, University of Colorado Boulder

Health literacy is a critical issue for our rapidly aging population. Older adults age 65 and above have the lowest health literacy level of all adult age groups in the U.S.. In this study, I developed an innovative, senior-friendly, theory-driven multimedia interactive eHealth tutorial -- a Firefox Extension, for aging groups to access and use reliable health information on the Internet. At the same time, I designed user studies to make comparisons with traditional tutroials(like paper-based and website-based) and determine the feasibility of older adults using this tutorial to improve their eHealth literacy.

Contact-free Camera Measurements of Heart Rate under Practical Scenario

Institute for Information Processing, Leibniz University Hannover, Germany

Unlike the traditional HR measurements based on ECG devices, the computer-aided contact-free HR detection methods are generally more convenient, flexible and practical. However, current frameworks are only applicable under rigorous experimental contexts, instead of realistic situations, where HR detection is more meaningful and necessary. In this study, I first implemented the current algorithm framework and improve the current algorithm framework by deploying DLIB method to detect region of interest(ROI) and two-way facial tracking to make sure the effectiveness of ROI. Secondly, new datasets are collected under designed practical scenarios and I analyzed our self-recorded materials qualitatively to conclude a guideline for further studies. Lastly, a ROI-selecting method specifically for head rotation situation was proposed under the direction of the guideline.

Recommendation System Implementation and Visualization Based on Yelp Data

University of Colorado Boulder

In this study, we used machine learning techniques to build a recommendation system based on Yelp Dataset. We deployed machine learning methods to build a recommendation system based on the prediction of user's rating for business (Pyhton, SQL) and the result of Collaborative Filtering algorithm was the baseline. We also blended several machine learning models by assigning weights and achieved best performance(AUC = 0.82).

Project Report | Code

With various features extracted from Yelp data, we conducted feature selection, best split and finally built a Decision Tree model which predicts users’ rating based on those features. Our visualization includes the complete process of this typical machine learning method, which provides insights about the inner mechanism of how the rating prediction is conducted.

Repeat Buyer Prediction Based on User Online Shopping Logs

University of Colorado Boulder

In this project, we developed a framework to predict whether a cus- tomer promoted by a big promotion(like "Double 11") will become a repeat buyer or not based on the dataset provided by Tmall.com. In terms of feature engineering, we extracted 129 features(user demographic features, user behavioral features, merchant-related features, user-merchant interaction features, big promotion features, slope features, similarity features, PCA features) and conducted feature ranking analysis. Our final AUC result rannked top 25% in the competition panel.
