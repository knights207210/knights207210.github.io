Visual Design

Posted by Xu on Oct 20, 2019

In this bolg, I'm going to discuss about visual design based on the following three readings: Chapter 4 from Organization and Visual Structure, Chapter 6 from Arranging Elements and Information Visualization: Perception for Design .

Organization and Visual Structure

From the reading, one of the most surprising thing I learnt is about symmetry and asymmetry. From what I orginally expected, a good design should be as symmetry as possible but it seems that this is totally not ture. An asymmetry design, for example, an interface with nicely organized blanks, can also be a good design. With this idea, I find that for our project "DogChat", we can also have some asymmetry design, which could make our interface more innovative.

Arranging Elements

One of the most useful points I learnt from this reading is positions of visual elements totally affect the design logic. I never realized this point before even I have used tons of visual interfaces. Right now, looking back at all these interfaces, some experiences strongly support this point. For example, weChat, one of my main social network app, always has "go back" button at the top left corner while "go forward" button at the top right corner. The position of these two buttons totally match with weChat's back-forward logic: users need to swipe left to go back and right to go forward. This can also be applied in our "DogChat" design as well.

Information Visualization: Perception for Design

The theories I learnt from this reading are really beneficial for me to understand how to make design elements standout/ignorant, to make important elements better grasp users eyes. The whole reading clearly elaborates how to make the whole information retrival process easier for users. In our "DogChat" design, we will follow these theories and suggestions. For example, the applications of different shapes, colors and textures can be useful for making our main function "Listening to your dog" standout other than thoes archtectural elements.
