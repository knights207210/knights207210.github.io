Visual Design

Posted by Xu on Oct 20, 2019

In this bolg, I'm going to talk about usability with the example of heating water using the glass stove. The stove generally has four sections and each of them can be used to heat, totally depends on users' needs.

Stoves

Gulf of execution & Gulf of evaluation

How the gulf of execution and gulf of evaluation could lead to problems when performing this heating water task?

As for gulf of execution, users see the solution to this problem as simply turning switch. However, in reality, to heat a pot of water, several actions must be taken:

1. Choose a certain position and put the water pot on.
2. Find the correct switch matching to the position.
3. Choose the heating degree and turn the switch to that degree.

As we can see, this difference between the user's perceived execution actions and the required actions makes the whole task performing more difficult, causing higher chance of failure.

As for gulf of evaluation, stoves' signal lights can be a good point to discuss about. Each position has a corresponding light, which could indicate the degree of heat at that position. In this case, if users turn the correct switch, the light to the corresponding position will be lightened up and this can be directly percived by users. If the signal lights cannot work, the "gulf of evaluation" will be typical in leading to problems performing this task.

Nielsen's heuristics

Based on previous explaination about gulf of execution and gulf of evaluation, following of the heuristics might fail for this task of heating water:

1. Visibility of system status:
In terms of gulf of evaluation, if the mentioned signal lights cannot work, users will have no other ways to get visual feedback about whether they turned the correct switch.

2. Recognition rather than recall:
In terms of gulf of execution, users need to find out which switch to turn at the beginning of the task. However, if the stoves fail to provide a good match between the switches and positions, this task will fail very easily.
